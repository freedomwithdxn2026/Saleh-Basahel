## Scope

- Memo date: 15 May 2026.
- Audience: all DXN Middle East distributors.
- Subject: strict compliance with DXN Rules & Regulations.
- Signatory: Sheby Philip, Regional Marketing Director, DXN Middle East.

## Core Rule

DXN-related activity must be conducted only through authorized and approved DXN platforms, systems, and communication channels.

## Clause 10.5 Risk

The memo cites DXN Rules & Regulations Clause 10.5. It prohibits a distributor from soliciting or persuading another distributor to promote, sell, or buy products or services that are not offered by DXN. The memo says violation can cause irreparable harm to DXN and may lead to disciplinary action and/or legal action.

## Prohibited Activities

Treat these as high-risk unless the user provides prior written approval from DXN management:

- Using unauthorized third-party websites, applications, software, online platforms, digital systems, or external services to promote, support, boost, or operate DXN business activity.
- Promoting or introducing non-DXN products, services, businesses, investment schemes, or marketing programs to DXN members or distributors.
- Using DXN meetings, seminars, events, social media groups, communication channels, or distributor networks for external business promotion.
- Creating unofficial websites, systems, online registration tools, promotional platforms, training portals, or digital services that represent DXN.
- Doing anything that may confuse distributors, misrepresent DXN, or negatively affect DXN business operations, policies, reputation, or marketing structure.

## Practical Guidance For Future Work

- Before building a DXN website, app, landing page, registration flow, training portal, CRM, or automation, ask whether it is officially approved or positioned as independent personal support that does not represent DXN.
- Prefer official DXN links, official DXN registration channels, and approved DXN materials.
- For personal distributor content, use clear disclaimers that the material is not an official DXN platform when appropriate, but do not assume a disclaimer is enough to satisfy the memo.
- Do not create tools that look like official DXN systems without written authorization.
- Do not mix DXN audiences with unrelated business, investment, or marketing offers.
- If the user wants an external system anyway, flag that written DXN management approval should be obtained first.
