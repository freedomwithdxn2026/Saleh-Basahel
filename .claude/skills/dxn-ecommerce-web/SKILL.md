# DXN Ecommerce Web

Build the usable selling or operations surface first. DXN web work should help visitors buy, contact, register interest, or manage distributor tasks quickly.

## Product And Sales UX

- Lead with real product categories, product photos, price/availability, and a clear next action.
- Make WhatsApp/contact actions obvious where direct sales are the business workflow.
- Support bilingual or localized copy when the audience requires it.
- For product pages, include ingredients/usage only when sourced from official material.
- Keep medical disclaimers near wellness/product content when health-related claims appear.
- Keep income/opportunity pages grounded in process and support, not guarantees.

## App Surfaces

For storefronts, prioritize:

- searchable catalog
- product category filters
- cart or inquiry list
- contact/checkout path
- order status
- trust/support signals

For distributor/admin dashboards, prioritize:

- lead pipeline
- customer/order table
- inventory/product status
- follow-up reminders
- sales and repeat-purchase metrics
- team/member status where authorized

## Implementation Workflow

1. Inspect the existing stack and design conventions.
2. Identify the first-screen job: sell a product, collect a lead, manage work, or report status.
3. Build responsive mobile-first flows because social and WhatsApp traffic are likely.
4. Test forms, product cards, tables, and navigation at mobile and desktop widths.
5. Protect payment, order, member, and customer data with secure defaults.

## Copy Rules

- Use concrete product and workflow language.
- Avoid exaggerated cure, weight-loss, disease-treatment, or guaranteed-income claims.
- Prefer "contact", "learn", "order", "register interest", and "follow up" actions over hype.
